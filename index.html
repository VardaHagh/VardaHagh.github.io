Varda F. Hagh

Postdoctoral Scholar, James Frank Institue,

University of Chicago

I am currently a Postdoctoral Scholar in the James Franck Institute at the University of Chicago and a member of the Simons Collaboration on Cracking the Glass Problem, working jointly with Professors Sidney R. Nagel (University of Chicago), Lisa Manning (Syracuse University), Eric Corwin (University of Oregon), and Andrea Liu (University of Pennsylvania). I am also affiliated with the University of Oregon and have spent a year of my postdoctoral work at their physics department.

In my postdoctoral work, I have studied various fundamental aspects of Soft Matter, including the mechanics of the jamming transition, memory formation in glasses, and training extraordinary function into soft materials. Training is a new and exciting paradigm in the manufacturing of materials-by-design that makes use of the inherent complexity and versatility of amorphous solids. I am particularly interested in the training of out-of-equilibrium amorphous solids such as glasses, granular media, proteins, and biopolymer networks since they are exceptionally versatile and are able to retain a memory of the changes they undergo.

I completed my Ph.D. work at Arizona State University under the supervision of Professor Michael Thorpe on optimization of the mechanical response in elastic solids.
